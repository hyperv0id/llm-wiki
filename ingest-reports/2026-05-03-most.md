# Ingest Report: MoST (KDD 2026)

**Source**: MoST: A Foundation Model for Multi-modality Spatio-temporal Traffic Prediction
**Authors**: Ronghui Xu, Jihao Chen, Jingdong Tian, Chenjuan Guo, Bin Yang (ECNU)
**DOI**: 10.1145/3770854.3780162

## Created

- **wiki/source-most.md** — WHY: Source-summary page required for every ingested paper. Summarizes MoST's two-module architecture (Multi-Modality Refinement + Spatio-Temporal Prediction), SNR-based modality selection, and zero-shot results across five datasets.

- **wiki/most.md** — WHY: Entity page for the MoST model. MoST is the first multi-modality spatio-temporal foundation model for traffic prediction, a significant milestone distinct from prior single-modal foundation models (OpenCity) and task-specific models (ConFormer, IGSTGNN). Includes architecture details, key results table, and comparison with related models.

- **wiki/multi-modality-refinement.md** — WHY: Technique page for the SNR-based modality selection mechanism. This is MoST's core innovation for handling variable modality availability and quality across cities. The SNR formulation (inspired by Shannon's second law) and Gumbel-Sigmoid differentiable gating are novel contributions not present in any existing wiki page.

- **wiki/multi-modality-guided-spatial-expert.md** — WHY: Technique page for the spatial expert mechanism with MoE routing. Distinct from FaST's HA-MoE (which uses MoE for feature transformation), MoST uses MoE for spatial dependency modeling with modality-shared and routed experts. The top-k neighbor cross-attention design is a key architectural choice for generalization.

- **wiki/spatio-temporal-foundation-model.md** — WHY: Concept page establishing the emerging paradigm of spatio-temporal foundation models. Bridges existing pages (OpenCity, TimesFM, Chronos) with MoST as the first multi-modal entry. Provides a framework for understanding the distinction between ST foundation models and general TS foundation models.

## Modified

- **wiki/traffic-forecasting.md** — WHY: Added "Foundation Model" subsection under Methods, introducing MoST as the first multi-modality ST foundation model. This fills a gap in the methods taxonomy that previously covered classical, graph-based, transformer, frequency-domain, periodicity-decoupled, accident-aware, incident-guided, and large-scale approaches but not foundation models.

- **wiki/multimodal-time-series-forecasting.md** — WHY: Added MoST section comparing it with UniCA and ChannelMTS. MoST represents a third paradigm for multimodal TS forecasting: native multi-modal training (vs. UniCA's TSFM adaptation and ChannelMTS's domain-specific design). Updated source_count from 2 to 3.

- **wiki/large-scale-spatial-temporal-graph.md** — WHY: Added "基础模型方法" section showing how MoST addresses large-scale ST through zero-shot transfer and localized spatial modeling (top-k neighbors). Complements FaST's efficiency-focused approach with a generalization-focused alternative. Updated source_count from 3 to 4.

- **wiki/mixture-of-experts.md** — WHY: Added MoST's spatial expert MoE as a second application of MoE in ST prediction (alongside FaST's HA-MoE). Highlights the architectural difference: MoST uses sparse Top-1 routing for spatial modeling vs. FaST's dense MoE for feature transformation. Updated source_count from 2 to 3.

- **wiki/timesfm.md** — WHY: Added cross-reference to MoST. Both are foundation models but in different domains (general TS vs. spatio-temporal traffic). No citation added (informational link only).

- **wiki/chronos.md** — WHY: Added cross-reference to MoST. Same rationale as TimesFM. No citation added.

- **wiki/unified-covariate-adaptation.md** — WHY: Added cross-reference to MoST and citation. UniCA adapts existing TSFMs to handle multi-modality covariates; MoST trains natively multi-modal from scratch. This is a meaningful architectural contrast worth documenting. Updated source_count from 1 to 2.

- **wiki/index.md** — WHY: Added all five new pages to appropriate categories (Sources, Entities, Concepts, Techniques). Updated last_updated.

- **wiki/log.md** — WHY: Appended ingest entry per workflow requirements.
